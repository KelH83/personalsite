About Me

I have a strong ambition to make the switch to tech and as well as having a proactive approach to acquiring new skills and knowledge I am motivated by a passion for innovation and problem-solving. I have a curious mindset, a willingness to learn, adaptability, and the ability to embrace challenges as opportunities for growth. So far I have learned HTML, CSS, Javascript, Bootstrap, GIT and Github. I have touched upon JQuery, Python, SQL, Node and React. I am continuously improving and adding to my skill base via online courses and coding challenges.

I have been very fortunate to place in the final 3 in some of the code first girls challenges both of these utilising some of my strengths, namely creativity, thinking outside of the box and my ability to hyperfocus for extended periods.

My previous roles gave me valuable creative experience that has enabled me to create visually appealing projects. My time-management skills and previous leadership roles have enabled me to keep my team(s) on track during challenges and classes. I’m very much a helper and researcher, whenever someone asks a question I will come up with a detailed data-driven answer whether that’s as in the past “What new class would be the most profitable for the school” to more recently “Why is mediation for Education Health and Care Plans virtually impossible to access?”.

I'm a disability advocate, I myself am disabled (neurodiverse) and so is my daughter and many family members. Creating a better world for the next generation of neurodiverse people is something I feel strongly about. I volunteer for a local charity, helping parents navigate their way through various systems that are not geared up for disabled children (schools, hospitals, activities etc). I also have regular meetings with local government groups, NHS groups, mental health departments/charities etc to try and help them make things better for disabled people (particularly children).

I'm also a frequent research participant, helping medical professionals understand more about neurodiversity and enabling them to create better screening tools/assessments and offer better levels of help.

I love to game, if I'm not coding then I'm gaming, I love all types of games from Gears of war and Halo to Mario and the Sims.

I love animals and currently have 2 dogs, 2 cats and 2 snakes. Dog walking is great exercise but I always seem to have my aha moments then, stuck on a tricky bit of code, 9 times out of 10 the solution usually comes to me when I'm 2 miles from the house and can't test it out!
